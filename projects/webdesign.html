Web Design


I think that everyone should operate a website. It doesn't even have to be a particularly good website. Social media websites have stripped away a lot of the individuality that came with everyone operating their own little home in cyberspace.

Websites used to be a highly personal thing. Geocities websites were works of art, even if half of them were flooded with horrible gifs and looked ugly as all heck, even back then. But what they had, even if ugly, was personality. No longer can you browse the internet and find someone's homepage completely dedicated to a certain type of soup, for example. Their SEO wouldn't be good enough to rank them high on Google, and as such, no one would find their website.

In the past, this issue was solved with "web rings" - a big group of websites whose owners would all agree to link to each other. This allowed people who browsed one site to find others that they might like, a sort of personalized recommendation by the owner. But these don't exist anymore - or if they do, they exist sparsely, only on hobbyist nerd sites like mine. But these days, SEO rules all, and its for the worse.

No longer can you find homepages or personal fan pages on the net. A Google search will never help you find the page you're reading right now, unless you know exactly what you're looking for. It's no longer possible to stumble upon small, unknown-to-most websites.

But as they say - you should lead by example. Every single page on this website was crafted from scrath by hand. Want to check out the computers that aided me in writing this website? Click here! On top of that, this website is active in a web ring! If you'd like to join, I'd be happy to link to your site. Feel free to Contact Me! Because that's the beauty of the web. Any person can load a text editor on their computer, or even phone, and make a website. It can be published for anyone to see in a matter of minutes. There are hundreds of free web hosts that can help you re-create what Geocities used to be, such as NeoCities! (You should give them a visit, and maybe even make a website!)

I'm in support of bringing the internet back to its roots. I'm sick of browsing only a select few shiny websites every day. I'd like to check out websites made by people I actually care about. I want to visit someone's homepage and see what updates they've made recently, what they've tweaked, how their homepage looks. And I decided to join that revolution by turning my homepage into a part of it.
