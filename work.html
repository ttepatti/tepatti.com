NEC PC-9801
The PC-9801 was manufactured from 1982 to 2000 (Almost twenty years!!!) The machines are amazing and have one of the largest libraries of classic Japanese home-made ('doujin') games. Everything from Touhou to various early visual novels were available on these machines, making them great for collecting and exploring early examples of gaming in Japan.
NEC PC-8800
The NEC PC-8800 is the 9801's grandpa. This machine was released in 1981 and featured the Zilog Z80, the same 8-bit processor you'd find in a TI-84 calculator today.

Don't let that dissuade you, though - the PC-8800 is an awesome machine with a huge library of classic home-made ('doujin') Japanese games to boot.
Sharp X68000
Another amazing Japanese computer. Futuristic looks, an amazing library of games, and an aesthetic thats hard to forget.
Fujitsu FM TOWNS II
How can you say no to that amazing front-mounted CD drive? The gray and green aesthetics are amazing, and this computer has the history and user support to back it up.
